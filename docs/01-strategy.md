## The thesis

Most professional services firms — the $5M to $100M consulting, law, accounting, MSP, financial advisory, A/E, recruiting, and agency firms — grow on **relationships and reputation**, not on funnels and cold outreach.

Every GTM framework built in the last 40 years was designed for **product companies launching to strangers**. PS firms have been borrowing those playbooks and wondering why they don't work. The problem is structural — those playbooks optimize for a buyer who doesn't know you yet. PS firms grow from buyers who already do.

So PS firms sit on millions of dollars in dormant relationships ("the **Dead Zone**") that no SaaS GTM playbook is built to activate. Between **60% and 96%** of CRM contacts at the average PS firm have not been spoken to in 90+ days.

> **GTM for professional services is a system for activating the market you already own.**

That definition is **locked**. Do not paraphrase. Do not extend. Do not qualify. It appears in the Preface, Chapter 1, and Closing of the manuscript, and on the back cover.

## The locked vocabulary (DO NOT change without coordination)

These twelve elements are permanent. Read before writing any copy. Return to confirm before locking any chapter or microsite section.

### Element 1 — The Definition

> **GTM for professional services is a system for activating the market you already own.**

Appears in Preface, Chapter 1, Closing, back cover. Never paraphrase. Never extend.

### Element 2 — The Formula

> **Signal + Proof + Context = Response, Not Pitch**

Displayed centered, on its own line, slightly larger type. Appears in every chapter at least once. No variation in typography or punctuation.

### Element 3 — The Five Truths

I. Your next client already knows you.
II. Timing beats targeting.
III. Reputation is a GTM channel.
IV. The Dead Zone is your biggest GTM asset.
V. New relationships are earned through relevance.

Always Roman numerals. Always this order. Language never changes.

### Element 4 — The Five Orbits

| Symbol | Name | What it is |
|---|---|---|
| ⊙ 01 | **Core Proof** | Top 15–20 relationships. Refer without being asked. The Core expressed outward. |
| ⊙ 02 | **Active** | Spoken in last 90 days with mutual value exchanged. |
| ⊙ 03 | **Dead Zone** | 60–96% of CRM. Dormant, not dead. Biggest GTM asset. |
| ⊙ 04 | **Warm Adjacency** | One degree removed. Borrowed trust. 7-day decay window. |
| ⊙ 05 | **New Gravity** | Cold outbound, events, inbound. Most expensive orbit. |

The `⊙` symbol precedes every orbit heading.
Orbit 03 is **always** "Dead Zone." Never "dormant contacts," "inactive leads," or "lapsed relationships."

### Element 5 — The Five Layers

> DISCOVER · PROVE · DESIGN · ACTIVATE · COMPOUND

Always small caps. Always this sequence. Never called "steps," "phases," or "stages." They are **layers**.

### Element 6 — The Three Laws

| | |
|---|---|
| **First Law** | Proof before pitch. |
| **Second Law** | Relationships before revenue. |
| **Third Law** | Signal before message. |

Always **"law."** Never rule, principle, or guideline. Sequence never changes.

### Element 7 — The Beachhead Principle

> Pick the one segment where your proof is deepest, your signals are most frequent, and your relationships are warmest. Win there completely before expanding.

Three criteria always in this order: proof depth, signal frequency, relationship warmth.

### Element 8 — Win/Loss Intelligence

A **quarterly practice**. Not one-time. Five questions. Three outputs:

1. One ICP refinement
2. One signal matrix update
3. One Orbit 03 reactivation list

Always "practice." Quarterly cadence fixed.

### Element 9 — Four Entry Points to Discovery

1. Mabbly AI reactivation
2. Book research application
3. Referral introduction
4. Inbound inquiry

Always this order. Discovery Session always described as **sixty minutes, free, no pitch**.

### Element 10 — Mabbly AI Three Conditions

All three must be present. Stated plainly. Not softened.

1. Sender has personal relationship context with the contact
2. Contact's company has an active public digital presence
3. Sender has a content library of at least five to ten pieces

### Element 11 — The MAP (Market Activation Profile)

Every Discovery Session produces a MAP. **Not a proposal. Not a deck. An artifact.** Delivered within 24 hours. **Twelve fields:**

1. **The Core** (purpose + niche, Evernote Test applied)
2. Session Voice (5 verbatim phrases)
3. Corporate Objectives
4. Core Values
5. ICP Lock
6. Beachhead Segment
7. Spectrum Position
8. Dead Zone Estimate
9. Orbit Priority
10. Primary Signal Type
11. Vision State
12. Competitive Landscape Signal

Each field carries a completion percentage.

> A proposal asks for something. The MAP gives something.

### Element 12 — The Core

> The Core is the center of the Five Orbits. It is the intersection of why your firm exists and who you serve better than anyone else.

Two halves: **Purpose** (why the firm exists) + **Niche** (who it serves better than anyone). Both statable in one sentence.

If managing partner and head of BD give different answers, **The Core is not set.**

Orbit 01 is called Core Proof because it is The Core expressed outward. The Beachhead Principle (Element 7) is how a firm finds its niche.

"The Core" always **capitalized**. Never hyphenated. Never abbreviated.

## Strategic principles (locked April 26 2026)

These are the rules every page edit must respect.

### 1. Verified facts only

See above. No fabricated stats. Where data is unverified, mark `[SOURCE PENDING]` and do not display until confirmed.

### 2. Native vocabulary per vertical

Each vertical reads native to its own world. Book IP terms (Dead Zone, Five Orbits, RROS) preserved with first-use translation per vertical.

### 3. Single CTA per page

No competing offers. Hero = single CTA. Sticky = same CTA. Final = same CTA. Per-vertical naming, never per-page swap.

### 4. Confidentiality over scarcity

"30 firms in / 70 spots remain" was replaced with **"Confidential. Benchmarked against peer firms."** Law and accounting partners reject scarcity tactics. Confidentiality is the trust signal.

### 5. Industry Reality data, sources cited

Every per-vertical stat marks its source (IBISWorld, AICPA, ABA, AESC, etc.). Where data is unverified, mark `[SOURCE PENDING]` and do not display.

### 6. Research framing, not sales pitch

The MAP is a **research artifact**, not a sales diagnostic. Visitor is a **contributor**, not a lead. Every CTA reads as research collaboration. "No pitch" appears in CTA copy explicitly. Each section ends with a question to the visitor (Observed → Hypothesis → **Question**).

### 7. Trust pattern: full reveal, no blur/lock/gate

V10 explicitly removed the email gate. PS firm partners reject manipulation. The blurred-lock pattern works for SaaS but actively damages trust with this audience. The MAP is fully visible. Email is **optional save**, never a wall.

The optimization metric is **schedule-meeting click**, not email capture. Trust converts to calls.

## Voice guide (project-wide)

### How Adam sounds

- Third-person analytical but warm, never clinical
- "After interviewing hundreds of PS leaders, I found..."
- Precise with numbers — never "significant improvement," always "8.1% vs 1.3%"
- Connects individual stories to systemic patterns
- Sentence length: medium. Cadenced. Building toward a conclusion.

**Adam pattern: assertion + immediate evidence. Never floats a claim without proof.**

### How Richard sounds

- First person, direct, no hedging
- Specific details (names, numbers, dates, cities)
- Self-deprecating once per chapter (the Richard line)
- Never lectures — compares notes
- Sentence length: short. Punchy. Scene-driven.

**Richard pattern: opens with a scene. Always grounds frameworks in lived experience.**

### Words to never use

- "leverage" (as a verb)
- "navigate"
- "activate them in order"
- "systematic relationship infrastructure"
- "Beta" (unfinished framework implication) → use "Early Access"
- "seats" (scarcity tactic) → use "approved by application"

### Tone rules

- No hedging
- No corporate-speak
- Numbers always exact and sourced
- Every framework reference uses the locked term verbatim
- Every CTA tied to research collaboration, not extraction
